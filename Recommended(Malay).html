Kassim Mustafa

Restoran Kassim Mustafa Sdn. Bhd. a household name in Penang today which was established and incorporated in the early 1980's was created to provide traditional Indian Muslim Cuisine for its customers from all walks of life, for both amateurs and professionals. Customers who patronize Kassim Mustafa outlets today will have a golden opportunity to experience exciting Authentic traditional Indian Muslim Cuisine whereby customers will be able to tantalize their taste buds with the exquisite flavors of sumptuous Indian Muslim Curries. Eatables that will make customers forget that they are on diet.


The said Restaurant before it became a house hold name in Penang today started its humble business along the corners of King street in the year 1980. At that time the sole proprietor of the said business today Encik Salim @ Saleem Bin Manshoor (PJM) who is also the managing director of the organization was an assistant to his uncle so called Kassim Mustafa. There they formed a small food stall popularly known at that time as "Gerai Nasi Dhalca" along King Street, Penang.

You can locate the restaurant at 126 and 128, Ground Floor, Jalan Mayang Pasir, Taman Sri Tunas, 11950 Bayan Lepas, Pulau Pinang, Malaysia. Its operating hours are from 6.30 PM to 11.30PM on Tuesday to Sunday. You can also contact them by this number +6016-425 6808.

Sup Hameed

Sup Hameed is knowned for its special and only lamb soup and nasi kandar.


Sup Hameed is renowned for its soups, not just for the wide variety that it offers, but also for the uniqueness of their tastes. Judging on a superficial level will probably do their soups injustice because they aren’t starchy or thick, and look, in fact, like any other soup would; but what distinguishes their soups is their characteristic taste. Most willv have their soup served with bread, the roti benggali, which is handmade. If you’re feeling adventurous, they have the sup torpedo, which is the bull’s penis soup. They also serve the usual mamak fare, such as nasi kandar, nasi lemak and roti canai.

You can locate the restaurant at 48 Jln Penang | Chinatown, George Town, Penang Island 10200 Pulau Pinang, Penang, Malaysia. Its operating hours are from 12.00 AM to 11.59PM daily. You can also contact them by this number +604-261 8007

Taha’s Corner

Spanning more than 35 years, this family-run stall has been churning out local delights such as roti canai, roti jala and assorted snacks at the junction of Penang Street and Bishop Street. Never pull the plug off looking at the unassuming backdrop, it’s full of flavours. Their roti jala is light and ethereal, which complements the generously spiced chicken and beef curries. Both the meats just falls apart with the mildest touch and slides down your throat like melted butter. Accompany your meal with a glass of lathered tea, which enhances the spicy aftertaste of the curries



The roti Jala Is best serverd with a glass of Teh Tarik.The price of the food is quite cheap as the price range is around MYR 1 -MYR 8. The Shop is located at At the right hand side of Penang Street. Situated near the junction between Penang Street and Bishop Street and opens daily at around 6.30am till 3pm

Kuala Pulau Betong (Kunita Stall)

Lekor or Keropok lekor is a traditional Malay fish cracker snack originated from the state of Terengganu, Malaysia. It is also called fish sausage, fish stick, or fish fritters. It is made from fish and sago flour and seasoned with salt and sugar.

This unassuming small house at Balik Pulau has baffled many straightaway; you’ll feel like stepping into Terengganu or Kelantan as three women are stocking up a reputation for delivering the best Keropok Lekors in Penang. As early as 6 am the preparations get going and served at a nearby stall in the late afternoon. Unlike the crispier ones you get from the east coast, the deep-fried lekor here is much soft, chewy and packed with strong fishy flavours and accompanied with the best mate – chili sauce. It's sinfully addictive; you’ll just gobble down as many batons as you can while admiring the untouched nature of Balik Pulau.
